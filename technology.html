The Pultrusion Process

A continuous, automated manufacturing method for producing constant cross-section composite profiles with exceptional consistency.

01

Creel Racks

Continuous glass fiber reinforcements are pulled from spools. This initial stage ensures precise tension and alignment of fibers before they enter the resin system, laying the foundation for structural integrity.

02

Resin Impregnation

Fibers are guided through a customized resin bath, ensuring complete wet-out. Our proprietary Polyurethane resin system bonds molecularly with the glass fibers, creating a composite material with superior durability.

03

Heated Die Forming

The wetted fibers enter a heated steel die where the shape plays a critical role. Under controlled high temperatures, the thermoset reaction occurs, curing the profile instantly into its permanent solid form.

04

Precision Cutting

Upon exiting the puller mechanism, the fully cured profile is cut to precise, customizable lengths by a flying cut-off saw, ready for assembly or further finishing.
